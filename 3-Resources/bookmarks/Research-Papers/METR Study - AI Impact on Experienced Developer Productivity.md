# METR Study: AI Impact on Experienced Developer Productivity

**URL:** [Read](https://metr.org/blog/2025-07-10-early-2025-ai-experienced-os-dev-study/)
**Authors:** METR (Model Evaluation & Threat Research)
**Date Published:** 2025-07-10
**Captured:** 2026-03-26

---

## Summary

Randomized controlled study of 16 experienced open-source developers (repos averaging 22,000+ stars, 1M+ LOC) completing 246 real issues. Found that AI tools made developers 19% slower, despite developers believing they were 20% faster.

---

## Key Points

1. **19% slowdown**: Developers using AI tools (primarily Cursor Pro with Claude 3.5/3.7 Sonnet) took 19% longer to complete real issues
2. **Perception gap**: Developers expected 24% speedup and believed they got 20% speedup even after experiencing the slowdown
3. **Methodological rigor**: Real issues from developers' own repos, randomly assigned to AI/no-AI conditions, $150/hr compensation, screen recording

---

## Relevance

Critical counter-evidence to productivity claims from GitHub/Microsoft-funded studies. The perception-reality gap undermines all self-reported productivity research. Used as key evidence in [[3-Resources/articles/Opinion/ai-developer-productivity-critical-analysis|AI Developer Productivity Critical Analysis]].
