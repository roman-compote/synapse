# Anthropic: How AI Assistance Impacts the Formation of Coding Skills

**URL:** [Read](https://www.anthropic.com/research/AI-assistance-coding-skills)
**Authors:** Anthropic Research
**Date Published:** 2026-02 (approx)
**Captured:** 2026-03-26

---

## Summary

Randomized controlled trial with 52 software engineers (mostly junior) showing AI-assisted developers scored 17% lower on comprehension tests (50% vs 67%, p=0.01). The largest gap was in debugging skills. Speed advantage was ~2 minutes and not statistically significant. How AI is used matters: delegation = 40% scores, conceptual inquiry = 65%+.

---

## Key Points

1. **17% comprehension gap**: AI-assisted group averaged 50% vs 67% for manual coders (Cohen's d=0.738)
2. **Debugging most impacted**: Largest performance disparity appeared on debugging questions
3. **Mode of use matters**: Delegation patterns scored <40%, conceptual inquiry patterns scored 65%+
4. **No significant speed gain**: ~2 minutes faster, not statistically significant
5. **Recommendation**: Managers should think intentionally about how to deploy AI tools; cognitive struggle is important for mastery

---

## Relevance

Demonstrates that AI coding tools can impair skill formation in junior developers. The "how you use it" finding is critical for training and education. Used as key evidence in [[3-Resources/articles/Opinion/ai-developer-productivity-critical-analysis|AI Developer Productivity Critical Analysis]].
