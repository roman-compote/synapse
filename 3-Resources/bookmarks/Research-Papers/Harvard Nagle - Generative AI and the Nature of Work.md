# Harvard/Nagle: Generative AI and the Nature of Work

**URL:** [Read on SSRN](https://dx.doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.5007084)
**Authors:** Frank Nagle et al. (Harvard Business School / MIT)
**Date Published:** 2026 (working paper)
**Captured:** 2026-03-26

---

## Summary

Study of 187,000 open-source developers who received free GitHub Copilot access. Found coding time increased 12.4%, project management fell 24.9%, peer collaboration declined ~80%, and new language exposure grew ~22%. Junior developers showed largest gains. Author warns of "retreat away from teamwork" and calls cutting junior hiring a "profound strategic error."

---

## Key Points

1. **+12.4% coding time**: Developers shifted work mix toward hands-on coding
2. **-24.9% project management**: Less time on issue triage, PR review, support queues
3. **-80% peer collaboration**: Dramatic decline in human-to-human interaction
4. **+22% language exposure**: Low-cost experimentation with new languages/frameworks
5. **Junior devs benefit most**: Largest increase in core coding time for less experienced
6. **"Profound strategic error"**: Author explicitly warns against cutting junior hiring
